ARU × Change Please

A crowdfunding campaign raising £10,000 to fund barista training placements for people experiencing homelessness. This document maps out the strategy, the roles, and the events so everyone involved can lead their part.
Target£10,000
SupervisorDr Tabish Zaman
StatusPlanning

Start Here

This campaign started as a case study. A group of MBA students at ARU, supervised by Dr Tabish Zaman, studied Change Please Foundation, a B Corp that runs 12-week training programmes for people experiencing homelessness. They visited the operation and met graduates who had gone from rough sleeping to living-wage jobs, stable housing, and ongoing support. 82% of graduates stay employed, and 3,283 people have been through the programme.

300,000 households in England are still in the worst forms of homelessness, and without a route to stable income, people cycle through temporary support and end up back in the same situation. Change Please offers that route, through employment with dignity rather than temporary relief.

We decided this was worth more than a case study, and we are raising £10,000 to fund training placements through Change Please by telling this story and giving people a way to be part of it.

The Events

Two events, two purposes, one campaign

Pre-Launch: Film Screening + Iftar

The first time the campaign goes public, in front of around 40 to 60 students and faculty. The goal is to set the right tone, build early momentum, and generate content that carries into the public launch phase.

Run of Show (Tentative)
  • 1
    6:30pm - Doors open
    Guests arrive and settle in.
  • 2
    7:00pm - Film screening (15-30 min)
    Change Please film or video content. Format and length to be confirmed with Dr Tabish.
  • 3
    7:30pm - Dr Tabish speaks (10 min)
    Introduces the campaign, explains the module connection, sets the context, and opens the broader conversation around social enterprise naturally.
  • 4
    7:40pm - Campaign introduction (3 min)
    Brief introduction to the campaign and how people can support it. The JustGiving page and QR codes are there for anyone who wants to contribute.
  • 5
    7:45pm - Iftar feast begins
    Informal networking, conversations, one-on-one asks from team members working the room for face-to-face asks.
  • 6
    8:30pm - Event wraps

Formal Launch: External Event

A curated event for around 30 to 50 people. The room will include entrepreneurs, EiRs, faculty, press, and professional contacts from across the network.

Run of Show (Tentative)
  • 1
    6:00pm - Doors open
    Guests arrive and settle in.
  • 2
    6:30pm - Campaign lead or Dr Tabish opens (5 min)
    Brief context for the room.
  • 3
    6:35pm - Change Please speaker (15-20 min)
    A Change Please representative shares how the model works, what it's achieved, and who it's helped. Could be a programme lead, a graduate, or a pre-recorded message if in-person isn't possible.
  • 4
    6:55pm - Q&A (10 min)
  • 5
    7:05pm - Raffle draw or silent auction results
    Depends on what prizes are secured. Change Please might offer a coffee subscription prize.
  • 6
    7:15pm - Networking
    Informal conversations and connections.
  • 7
    8:00pm - Event wraps
CA

Catering

What Needs to Happen

Both events require catering. The university catering team needs to be briefed early so they can accommodate the right format, dietary requirements, and headcount.

Plan
  • 1
    Confirm event funding
    Check whether university event funding covers catering for either or both events.
  • 2
    Brief university catering
    Pre-launch is an Iftar: no alcohol, water and juice only, halal food as standard with vegetarian and vegan options alongside. Formal launch is professional finger food and canapés with soft drinks, juice, and tea/coffee.
  • 3
    Dietary and allergen planning
    Both events: halal, vegetarian, and vegan options as standard. Allergen cards next to each dish. Include "Any dietary requirements?" in the RSVP.
  • 4
    Confirm headcount
    Pre-launch: around 40 to 60. Formal launch: around 30 to 50. Final numbers confirmed closer to the date based on RSVPs.

DD

Double or Divide

What We're Proposing

A viral donation mechanic where someone donates, then challenges two friends to either match their donation or split it between them. A £20 donation turns into two more. Each person either gives £20 (match) or £10 (split), then tags two more. One donation becomes three, three become nine, and it compounds from there.

Plan
  • 1
    Create a shareable template
    A graphic or story template that explains the mechanic, includes the JustGiving link, and makes it easy to participate.
  • 2
    Deploy during the momentum phase
    Weeks 10-11, once there's enough traction that people feel comfortable joining in.
